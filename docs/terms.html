2. What the Civic Transparency Toolkit Is

The Civic Transparency Toolkit is a free, open-source desktop application that uses the Anthropic Claude API to run a nine-agent editorial pipeline for civic reporting. It searches public government sources, detects story leads, performs adversarial verification, and produces draft stories for human review. The Toolkit is a community tool — it is not a news organization, not a journalism credential, and not a substitute for professional reporting.

5.1 Defamation Risk

AI language models can fabricate facts, misattribute statements, invent quotes, and mischaracterize public officials' positions. Publishing such fabrications may expose you to defamation claims. The Software's integrity checking and adversarial verification reduce but do not eliminate this risk. You must independently verify every factual claim, attribution, and quote before publication.

5.2 Plagiarism Risk

AI models synthesize information from their training data, which includes published journalism. The Software includes anti-plagiarism protocols, but no automated system can guarantee that output does not inadvertently reproduce or closely paraphrase another outlet's original reporting.

5.3 AI Hallucination

AI models can produce outputs that appear authoritative but contain fabricated information, including invented meeting dates, fabricated quotes, nonexistent government documents, and incorrect characterizations of policy changes.

5.4 Not a Journalism Credential

Using this Software does not make you a journalist, does not confer press credentials, and does not grant legal privileges associated with professional journalism.

5.5 Automated Pipeline Risk

Unlike the prompt-only Civic Newsroom, this toolkit automates the pipeline execution. Multiple AI agents process your sources in sequence. Errors in early stages may cascade through the pipeline, compounding inaccuracies in the final output. Always review the complete output, not just the final stories.
