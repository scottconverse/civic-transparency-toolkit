Civic Transparency Toolkit

A free desktop app that turns your city's public records into stories you can share with your neighbors

Since 2005, more than 2,500 U.S. newspapers have closed. City councils still meet. Budgets still get passed. Contracts still get awarded. But in most communities, nobody is reading any of it.

The Civic Transparency Toolkit is a desktop app that watches your city's public websites, finds the stories that matter, fact-checks them, and writes them up as plain-English articles you can share. One click to run. One click to export. Built-in safeguards against hallucination, plagiarism, and one-sided analysis.

What It Does

Six pipeline lanes, each designed for a different part of civic reporting.

Primary Lane
Daily Production
The workhorse. Scans your sources, finds 15–25 leads, expands the best into stories, fact-checks them, and rewrites them as clean, shareable articles. One button, four stages, done.
Investigation
Signal Intelligence
Looks for the dog that didn't bark. Scans community sources for patterns, then runs every signal through a four-gate adversarial verification before promoting it to a story.
On Demand
Ad-Hoc Story
Give it a topic — “What's happening with the new development on Main Street?” — and it does its own research, writes a complete story, and runs it through the fact-checker.
Full Pipeline
Combined Production
Runs Daily Production and Signal Intelligence back to back. The most thorough option for a weekly deep-dive into everything your local government is doing.
Deep Dive
Deep Investigation
Full pipeline plus standalone research. Every agent fires. Use this when you want to leave no stone unturned on a specific issue or time period.
Flexible
Single Prompt
Run any of the nine agents individually. Useful for one-off tasks — checking your legal rights, verifying a specific claim, or grounding a story in primary sources.

Built-In Safeguards

AI-assisted civic reporting fails in specific, predictable ways. This system prevents each of them architecturally.

Adversarial Verification
A mandatory four-gate protocol forces counter-narrative searches across 3+ platforms within a 90-day window. Contested claims can't advance without documented verification attempts.
Anti-Plagiarism Architecture
The Civic Grounding Protocol ensures every claim traces to primary public records. Other outlets' coverage can tell you what to look for — it can never be your source.
Integrity Auditing
Every story from any pipeline must pass an independent five-part audit before it reaches you. No agent can self-certify its own output as ready.
Suppression Ledger
Every killed story gets a record: why it was killed, what was missing, and what would reopen it. Nothing disappears silently.

Get Started in 5 Minutes

  1. Download the latest release and unzip it, or clone the repo and run python main.py
  2. Add your API key in Settings — get one free at console.anthropic.com (usage costs ~$0.10/run)
  3. Configure your city — name, state, and open records law
  4. Add sources — paste URLs for your city's council portal, news sites, school district, and community forums
  5. Pick a lane and press Run — the toolkit handles the rest
